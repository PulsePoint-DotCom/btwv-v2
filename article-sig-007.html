What we're seeing

Raycast has been quietly building one of the most devoted fanbases in the Mac ecosystem. Their launch window manager and clipboard history alone have earned cult status. Now they're layering AI on top in a way that feels inevitable in hindsight — why shouldn't your launcher also structure your data?

The mechanic is elegant: you highlight any text anywhere on screen, hit a global hotkey, and within seconds the AI returns structured output. A messy CSV pasted from a PDF becomes a clean JSON array. A paragraph of names and titles becomes a properly formatted table. The keyboard-first design means you never have to switch apps or copy-paste into ChatGPT.

The 289 Product Hunt votes in a single day is actually underselling the real adoption curve. That's organic spread, not a launch blast.

The pattern here is familiar: tools that eliminate a small, frequent friction point outperform tools that solve big but rare problems. Everyone needs to format data. Not everyone needs a full AI coding assistant. The frequency × simplicity equation is driving the adoption.
